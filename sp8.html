UI/UX

Menus and UI: their purpose:

Main Menu
The main menu was set to be a part of the first area for the game, the cottage. Immediately placing and immersing the player into the world.
As the menu is navigated a camera moves to various postions within the cottage, allowing elements such as the title of the game and a credits group photo to be diegetic.
Pressing play transitions the camera to where the player is supposed to start the level.

Pause Menu
Pauses the game while still showing the environment of the level.

HUD
A centered sprite that changes depending on if something is interactable or not, similar to our reference game SOMA.

IronWrought does not have support for doing all the goals the group had for the UI. I opted to not expand on existing systems of the engine which allowed faster prototyping and end results at the cost of flexibility. A scene in IronWrought can hold one set of UI elements: a canvas. The idea being that a scene has a purpose and the canvas is tied to that purpose. The workaround was to have all necessary canvases ready and swap which one the scene held to fit the state of the game. The sprite change on the hud is handled in a specific case through IronWrought's post master, there were different objects that could affect which sprite to display.

Monster: Attacking the Player

When the monster reaches the player, or perhaps the player bumps into the monster out of curiosity, the monster reacts by grabbing the player. I setup the process for the event. Positioning and orienting both the player and the monster to work with the monster's animation at the start of it. Teleporting the monster to a nearby spawnpoint once finished and leaving the player in where it was at the start.

Monster: Animations

Reused the AI animation controller from I AM to connect animations to the monster's various states.
Whenever the monster changed states (example: Patrol to Seek) the animation controller would receive a message with some data regarding the state.
The data most often being where between 2 animations the animation should start. For example Seek, running with arms behind or reaching forward, would pass a % value: currentDistanceToPlayer / closeToPlayerMinimum on first entering the state and during updates.

Other Contributions

Monster: Hearing Player Footsteps
The monster could hear both objects being thrown around and the player.
The monster received the players playStepSound message along with data describing either walk or sprint which determines the hearing range. Comparing the squared distance between the monster and sound origin to the squared hearing range.
